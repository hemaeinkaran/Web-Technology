Block and Inline Elements

Block - level elements


Block - level elements appear on the screen as if they have a carriage return or line break before and after them.
For example, the < p > , < h1 > , < h2 > , < h3 > , < h4 > , < h5 > , < h6 > , < ul > , < ol > , < dl > , < pre > , < hr / > , < blockquote > , and < address > elements are all block - level elements.
They all start on their own new lines, and anything that follows them appears on its own new line, too.


Block-Level Elements

Block-level elements always start on a new line. The <h1 > and <p > elements will not sit on the same line, whereas the inline elements flow with the rest of the text.

Inline elements


Inline elements, on the other hand, can appear within sentences and do not have to appear on new lines of their own.
The < b > , < i > , < u > , < em > , < strong > , < sup > , < sub > , < big > , < small > , < ins > , < del > , < code > ,
< cite > , < dfn > , < kbd > , and < var > elements are all inline elements.
